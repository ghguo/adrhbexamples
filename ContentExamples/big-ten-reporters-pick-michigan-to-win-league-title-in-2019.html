Big Ten reporters pick Michigan to win league title in 2019

Updated Jul 17, 2019; Posted Jul 17, 2019
By Aaron McMann | amcmann@mlive.com

Football reporters who cover the Big Ten have spoken, and a good chunk of them believe this is the year Michigan finally gets over the hump.

The Jim Harbaugh-led Wolverines are the favorites to win the Big Ten championship in 2019, according to the annual preseason poll conducted this week by Cleveland.com.

Thirty-four reporters took part in the poll, including one covering each of the 14 Big Ten teams. The league did away with a preseason media poll in 2011.

The results come just days before the Big Ten is set to hold its annual two-day media preview event, scheduled for Thursday and Friday in Chicago.

Michigan received a league-best 222 points, including 20 first-place votes to win the Big Ten East Division, upending two-time defending Big Ten champion Ohio State. The Buckeyes finished second in East voting, receiving 214 points and 14 first-place votes.

Michigan State checked in at No. 3 with 156 points, followed closely behind by Penn State at No. 4 with 154 points.

Steele's annual college football preview magazine is on newsstands now.

The West, however, is believed to be more of a crapshoot. Five of the seven teams on that side of the standings received at least one first-place vote, with Nebraska and Iowa each leading the way with 14. The Cornhuskers, with 198 points, narrowly squeezed by Iowa (194.5 points) to win the West thanks to down-ballot voting.

Other West teams to receive first-place votes: Wisconsin (4), which was picked to finish third in the West; Northwestern (1), picked to finish fourth; and Minnesota (1), picked to finish sixth.

Half of the 34 voters picked Michigan to win the Big Ten championship game, either beating Nebraska (8), Iowa (8) or Wisconsin (1). Another 14 voters picked Ohio State to win the league title game, while two voters predicted a West team to beat Michigan in Indianapolis.

The prediction -- Michigan winning the Big Ten -- seems to be a popular one nationally of late. Several preseason college football publications, including Phil Steele and Athlon Sports, both have Michigan winning the league and reaching the College Football Playoff in 2019.

The Wolverines return nine starters on offense, one that has a new offensive coordinator and direction, and five on defense. Under coordinator Don Brown, Michigan has boasted a top-three defense nationally each of the last three years.

Of course, those who participate in this poll aren’t always correct in their predictions. Last year, for example, Big Ten reporters unanimously picked Wisconsin to win the West, while Northwestern, the eventual division champion, was picked to finish third.

Additionally, voters have correctly picked the Big Ten champion just twice in the last nine years, though both instances came in 2017 and 2018 when Ohio State won the league title game.
